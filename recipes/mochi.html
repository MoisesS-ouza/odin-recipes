Mochi

Description

Mochi is a traditional Japanese food made from glutinous rice that is pounded into a sticky, elastic paste and then shaped into small round cakes. It has a chewy texture and a slightly sweet flavor. Mochi can be enjoyed on its own or with various fillings and toppings. They are commonly filled with red bean paste. Other ingredients include water, sugar and cornstarch. They can be molded into a desired shape.

It can be seen as Plain Mochi - Simple and unflavored, Daifuku - Filled with sweetened red bean paste, Mochi Ice Cream - Filled with ice cream, etc. Today, it's going to be Daifuku, a mochi filled with red bean paste.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sweetened red bean paste
  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon green tea powder; matcha
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch, for rolling out the dough

Steps

  1. Firstly, gather all the ingredients
  2. Wrap red beam paste in aluminum foil and place in the freezer until solid, wait about 1 hour
  3. Mix glutinous rice flour and green tea powder thoroughly in a microwave-safe-bowl
  4. Stir in water, then sugar; Mix until it's smooth
  5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for 3 minutes and 30 seconds
  6. Meanwhile, remove red bean paste from the freezer and divide into 8 balls. Set aside
  7. Remove rice flour mixture from the microwave. Stir and heat, covered, for another 15 to 30 seconds
  8. Dust a work surface with cornstarch. Roll about 2 tablespoons of hot rice flour mixture into a ball. Flatten the ball and place one ball of frozen red bean paste in the center. Pinch and press the dough around the bean paste until completely covered.
  9. Sprinkle with additional cornstarch and place mochi, seam-side down, in a paper muffin liner to prevent sticking.
  10. Repeat Step 6 to make remaining mochi.
  11. Enjoy!
